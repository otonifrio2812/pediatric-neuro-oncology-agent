# Pediatric neuro-oncology safety principles

This local knowledge-base file is intentionally conservative and is used only for retrieval-augmented decision support.

- AI output must not be treated as definitive diagnosis.
- Pediatric brain tumor management should be reviewed by a multidisciplinary team.
- Imaging-derived findings are auxiliary observations and require neuroradiology review.
- Brainstem, thalamic, spinal cord, and posterior fossa lesions require careful discussion of neurologic risk.
- Surgical goals may include tissue diagnosis, safe decompression, CSF diversion, or safe maximal resection depending on anatomy.
- GTR vs STR discussion must consider eloquent anatomy, cranial nerve nuclei, major vessels, fourth ventricle, and hydrocephalus.
